🔒 Security Checks

  • 1. HTTPS enabled? Your site should load over HTTPS (padlock icon in browser). Without it, browsers will warn visitors your site is "Not Secure." Google also ranks HTTPS sites higher.
  • 2. Security headers present? Headers like HSTS, Content-Security-Policy, and X-Frame-Options protect your site from common attacks. Most small business sites are missing all three.

📝 SEO Checks

  • 3. Title tag optimized? Your title tag is the #1 factor for Google click-through rates. It should be 50-60 characters, include your main keyword, and be unique for each page.
  • 4. Meta description set? This is the snippet Google shows under your link. Without one, Google picks random text from your page — usually not what you'd want.
  • 5. Images have alt text? Every image should have descriptive alt text. This helps Google understand your images and makes your site accessible to visually impaired visitors.
  • 6. robots.txt and sitemap.xml? These files help search engines crawl your site efficiently. Visit yoursite.com/robots.txt and yoursite.com/sitemap.xml — if you get a 404, they're missing.

⚡ Performance Checks

  • 7. Page loads in under 3 seconds? 53% of visitors leave if a mobile page takes over 3 seconds to load. Test yours at PageSpeed Insights.
  • 8. Mobile-friendly? Over 60% of web traffic is mobile. Your site needs a viewport meta tag and responsive design. Try loading it on your phone — if you need to pinch and zoom, there's a problem.

📢 Social & Sharing

  • 9. Open Graph tags set? When someone shares your link on Facebook, LinkedIn, or iMessage, OG tags control what image, title, and description appear. Without them, shared links look generic and unprofessional.
  • 10. Contact info easily findable? Your phone number, email, and address should be on every page — preferably in the header or footer. If someone has to hunt for how to contact you, they won't.
