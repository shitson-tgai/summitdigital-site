How Much Does a Website Audit Cost in 2026? (Complete Pricing Guide)

Updated March 2026 · 7 min read

If you've ever Googled "website audit," you've seen quotes ranging from $50 to $15,000. That's not helpful.

Here's the truth: most small businesses don't need a $5,000 audit. They need someone to tell them what's broken and how to fix it — clearly, quickly, and affordably.

Let's break down what you actually get at each price point.

Website Audit Pricing Tiers

Free — Automated Scanners

Examples: Google PageSpeed Insights, GTmetrix, our free checker

What you get: A quick snapshot of technical issues — load speed, missing meta tags, security headers, basic accessibility. Usually takes 30 seconds to a few minutes.

Good for: A first look. Understanding if you have problems worth fixing.

Limitations: No human analysis. No prioritization. No "here's exactly how to fix this." You get the problems, not the solutions.

$9–$50 — AI-Powered Comprehensive Audits

Examples: Summit Web Audit ($9), SiteChecker ($23/mo), SEOptimer ($19/mo)

What you get: A detailed report covering SEO, security, performance, accessibility, and mobile experience. 50+ checkpoints with specific fix instructions. PDF report you can hand to your developer.

Good for: Small businesses that want a clear action plan without spending hundreds.

This is the sweet spot for most small businesses. You get 90% of the value of a $500 audit at a fraction of the cost.

$100–$500 — Freelancer Audits

Examples: Upwork/Fiverr freelancers, small agencies

What you get: A human looks at your site and writes up findings. Usually covers SEO, basic technical issues, and some UX feedback. Turnaround: 3–7 days.

Good for: Businesses that want a human perspective and are willing to wait.

Watch out for: Quality varies wildly. Many freelancers just run the same free tools you could run yourself, then charge you for a nicely formatted PDF.

$500–$2,500 — Agency Audits

Examples: WebFX, local SEO agencies, digital marketing firms

What you get: In-depth analysis covering technical SEO, content strategy, competitor analysis, backlink profile, and conversion optimization. Usually 20–40 page report with recommendations. Turnaround: 1–3 weeks.

Good for: Established businesses with real traffic looking to optimize, or businesses preparing for a redesign.

The reality: If your site gets under 1,000 visits/month, most of this analysis is overkill. Fix the basics first.

$2,500–$15,000+ — Enterprise Audits

Examples: Large SEO firms, consulting agencies (Moz, SEMrush Pro services)

What you get: Everything above plus international SEO, site architecture overhaul, custom crawl analysis, content gap analysis, and ongoing consultation. May include implementation.

Good for: Large e-commerce sites, multi-location businesses, enterprises with complex technical stacks.

Not for: Most small businesses. Period.

What Most Small Businesses Actually Need

💡 The honest truth: If you're a local business, service provider, or small e-commerce shop, you need to know three things:
  1. What's broken on your site right now?
  2. What's the most important thing to fix first?
  3. How do I fix it (or what do I tell my developer)?

A $9 audit answers all three. A $5,000 audit also answers all three — it just includes a lot of analysis you'll never act on.

How to Decide What to Spend

Ask yourself:

  • "Do I know what's wrong with my site?" → No? Start with a free scan or $9 audit. Don't spend $500+ to discover the basics.
  • "Do I have a developer who can fix things?" → Yes? You just need the findings + instructions. An affordable audit is enough.
  • "Is my site driving significant revenue?" → If your site generates $10K+/month, a $500–$2,500 audit may have clear ROI. If it generates $0, start small.
  • "Am I worried about ADA or HIPAA compliance?" → A compliance-focused audit that costs $9–$50 today could prevent a $30,000+ lawsuit. Best ROI of any business expense.
