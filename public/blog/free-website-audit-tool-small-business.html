What Does a Website Audit Check?

A website audit is a comprehensive review of your site's technical health. Think of it like a check-up for your website. Here's what a good audit covers:

1. SEO (Search Engine Optimization)

This is how Google finds and ranks your site. An audit checks:

  • Title tags — Does every page have a unique, descriptive title?
  • Meta descriptions — These show up in Google search results. Missing them = lower click-through rates.
  • Heading structure — Proper H1, H2, H3 hierarchy helps Google understand your content.
  • Open Graph tags — These control how your site looks when shared on social media.
  • Canonical URLs — Prevents duplicate content issues.
  • robots.txt and sitemap.xml — Tells search engines which pages to index.

2. Security

Website security isn't just for e-commerce sites. Every business website needs:

  • SSL/HTTPS — The padlock icon. Without it, browsers show "Not Secure" to your visitors.
  • HSTS header — Forces browsers to always use HTTPS.
  • Content Security Policy — Prevents cross-site scripting (XSS) attacks.
  • X-Frame-Options — Prevents clickjacking attacks.
  • Referrer-Policy — Controls what information is shared when visitors click links.

3. Performance

Speed matters. Amazon found that every 100ms of extra load time costs 1% in sales. For a small business, a slow website means:

  • Higher bounce rates (visitors leave before the page loads)
  • Lower Google rankings (speed is a ranking factor)
  • Worse mobile experience (mobile users are less patient)

4. Accessibility

Accessibility isn't just the right thing to do — it's increasingly a legal requirement. An audit checks:

  • Image alt text — Screen readers need this to describe images to visually impaired users.
  • Viewport meta tag — Makes your site work on mobile devices.
  • Language attribute — Tells browsers and screen readers what language your content is in.

What to Do After Your Audit

  1. Fix critical issues first — SSL, broken pages, missing titles. These have the biggest impact.
  2. Address security headers — Most can be added with a few lines of configuration.
  3. Optimize meta tags — Write unique titles and descriptions for every page.
  4. Test on mobile — Open your site on your phone. Can you read it? Can you find your phone number?
  5. Re-scan monthly — Websites change. Run an audit at least once a month.

Who Needs a Website Audit?

If you answer "yes" to any of these, you need an audit:

  • Your website is more than 6 months old and hasn't been checked
  • You're not showing up on Google for your business name
  • Your site feels slow on mobile
  • You're not sure if your SSL certificate is working
  • You hired someone to build your site but aren't sure if they did it right
  • Your competitors seem to rank better than you
  • You've never heard of "security headers" or "meta descriptions"
