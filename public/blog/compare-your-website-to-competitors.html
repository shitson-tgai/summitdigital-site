What Gets Compared?

Our comparison tool runs 50+ checks on both websites simultaneously and shows you a head-to-head breakdown across four categories:

🔍 SEO

  • Title tags and meta descriptions
  • H1 heading structure
  • Open Graph tags (how your site looks on social media)
  • Canonical URLs
  • Structured data (Schema.org)
  • Image alt text coverage

🔒 Security

  • HTTPS and HSTS enforcement
  • Content Security Policy
  • X-Frame-Options (clickjacking protection)
  • X-Content-Type-Options
  • Referrer-Policy

♿ Accessibility

  • Language attribute on HTML
  • Skip navigation links
  • Form label coverage
  • ARIA landmarks and semantic HTML
  • Link text quality
  • Empty links and buttons

⚡ Performance

  • Page load time
  • Mobile viewport configuration

Why Compare Matters

Your website doesn't exist in a vacuum. When a potential customer is choosing between you and a competitor, they're comparing:

  1. Trust signals — Does the site feel secure? Do browsers show warnings?
  2. Speed — Which site loads faster? (Amazon found every 100ms of latency costs 1% in sales)
  3. Search visibility — Who shows up higher in Google for the same keywords?
  4. Accessibility — Can everyone use the site, including people with disabilities?

The average small business website scores 47/100 in our health checks. If your competitor scores 65, that's not just a number — it's a competitive advantage in Google's eyes.

How to Use the Results

After running a comparison, you'll see:

  • Winner banner — Immediately see who scores higher overall
  • Score cards — Both scores side by side with issue counts
  • Issue-by-issue table — Every check showing ✅ Pass or ❌ Issue for each site

Focus on the issues where you fail but your competitor passes. Those are your biggest opportunities — fixes that put you ahead.

What to Fix First

If you're behind, prioritize in this order:

  1. Security headers — Most impactful for trust and Google ranking signals. Often fixable in 5 minutes with a plugin or server config.
  2. SEO basics — Title tags, meta descriptions, and H1 headings directly affect your search visibility.
  3. Accessibility — ADA web accessibility lawsuits hit an all-time high of 8,600+ in 2024. The skip-nav and ARIA fixes are quick wins.
  4. Performance — If your site loads slower than your competitor's, you're losing mobile visitors.
