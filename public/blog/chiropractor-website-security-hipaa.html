Why Chiropractors Are Especially Vulnerable

Most chiropractic practices rely on their website for new patient acquisition. That means your site likely has:

  • Contact forms where patients describe their symptoms
  • Appointment request forms collecting names, phone numbers, and sometimes insurance info
  • Patient intake forms that collect health history before the first visit
  • Live chat widgets where patients share health concerns

Every one of these is a potential HIPAA violation if your website isn't properly secured.

The 7 Security Issues We Found Most Often

Here's what we found when scanning chiropractic websites:

1. Missing HSTS Header (68% of sites)

HSTS (HTTP Strict Transport Security) forces browsers to use encrypted HTTPS connections. Without it, patient data submitted through your forms could be intercepted. This is the single most common security gap we find.

2. No Content Security Policy (71% of sites)

A Content Security Policy prevents malicious scripts from being injected into your website. Without one, attackers could potentially capture form submissions — including patient data.

3. Missing Referrer-Policy (55% of sites)

Without this header, when a patient clicks a link on your site, the destination website can see the full URL they came from — potentially including patient-identifiable information in query parameters.

4. No Meta Description (45% of sites)

While not a security issue, missing meta descriptions mean Google creates its own snippet for your site in search results. You're losing control of your first impression with potential patients.

5. Missing Alt Text on Images (61% of sites)

Images without alt text hurt your SEO and make your site inaccessible to visually impaired visitors. Under the ADA, healthcare providers have an obligation to maintain accessible websites.

6. No Structured Data (78% of sites)

Structured data (Schema.org markup) helps Google understand your business — your hours, location, services, and reviews. Without it, you're invisible to rich search results that drive clicks.

7. Slow Load Times (34% of sites)

Sites loading in 5+ seconds lose 40% of visitors. For a chiropractic practice, that's potential patients clicking "back" and choosing your competitor instead.

What HIPAA Actually Requires for Your Website

HIPAA's Security Rule requires "appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards" for protected health information (PHI). For your website, this means:

  • SSL/TLS encryption — Your site must use HTTPS (the padlock icon)
  • HSTS header — Force all connections to use encryption
  • Secure form handling — Form data must be encrypted in transit and at rest
  • Access controls — Limit who can access submitted patient data
  • BAA with vendors — Your hosting provider, form handler, and email service need Business Associate Agreements
  • Security headers — CSP, X-Frame-Options, and Referrer-Policy protect against common attacks
💡 Important: Having an SSL certificate (HTTPS) is necessary but NOT sufficient. Many chiropractors think the padlock icon means they're HIPAA compliant. It doesn't. You need the full set of security headers to properly protect patient data.
