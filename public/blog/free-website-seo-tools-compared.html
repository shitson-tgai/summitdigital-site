5 Best Free Website Audit Tools for Small Business (2026 Comparison)

Your website has problems you don't know about. Missing meta tags, broken security headers, slow load times, accessibility gaps — any of these can cost you customers and tank your Google ranking.

The good news: there are free tools that find these issues in minutes. The bad news: most are built for developers, not business owners. We tested the 5 most popular free website audit tools and compared them on what matters to small businesses: ease of use, actionable advice, and comprehensiveness.

The 5 Tools, Reviewed

Best for Small Business

1. Summit Web Audit

Price: Free scan / $9 full audit · Time: 30 seconds · Signup: None

  • No signup required — instant results
  • Plain English explanations
  • Covers SEO, security, accessibility, performance
  • Health score out of 100
  • $9 full report with fix instructions
  • Free scan limited to 18 checks
  • Single page analysis (not full site crawl)
  • New tool — smaller community
Best for: Small business owners who want a quick, jargon-free health check with clear next steps. The only tool on this list designed specifically for non-technical users.

2. Google PageSpeed Insights

Price: Free · Time: 15–30 seconds · Signup: None

  • From Google — the authority on web performance
  • Core Web Vitals data
  • Mobile + desktop results
  • Free, no limits
  • Performance only — no SEO, security, or accessibility
  • Technical jargon heavy
  • Suggestions require developer knowledge
  • No overall "score" that's easy to understand
Best for: Checking page speed specifically. Great as a supplementary tool, but doesn't tell you about security, SEO, or accessibility issues.

3. GTmetrix

Price: Free (3 tests/day) · Time: 30–60 seconds · Signup: Optional

  • Detailed waterfall chart
  • Historical tracking (with account)
  • Clear performance grades
  • Performance focused only
  • Free tier limited to 3 tests/day
  • Paid plans start at $15/month
  • No security or SEO checks
Best for: Deep-diving into page load performance. Overkill for most small businesses but great for developers optimizing speed.

4. Screaming Frog SEO Spider

Price: Free (500 URLs) · Time: Minutes to hours · Signup: Desktop download

  • The most comprehensive crawler available
  • Finds broken links, redirects, duplicate content
  • Industry standard for SEO professionals
  • Requires download and installation
  • Extremely technical interface
  • Designed for SEO professionals, not business owners
  • No security or accessibility checks
Best for: SEO professionals doing full site audits. Not recommended for small business owners — the learning curve is steep.

5. Ahrefs Webmaster Tools

Price: Free · Time: Hours (full crawl) · Signup: Required + domain verification

  • Comprehensive site audit (100+ checks)
  • Backlink analysis included
  • Professional-grade SEO data
  • Ongoing monitoring
  • Account creation + domain verification required
  • Takes hours for initial crawl
  • Very technical — not beginner-friendly
  • No security header checks
Best for: Business owners who are comfortable with SEO tools and want ongoing monitoring. Powerful but requires investment of time to set up and learn.
