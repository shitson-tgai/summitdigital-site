What Is a Website Health Score? (And Why It Matters for Your Business)

March 28, 2026 · 6 min read

You get annual physicals. You check your car's dashboard lights. But when was the last time you checked the health of your website?

A website health score is a single number — typically 0 to 100 — that measures how well your website performs across the areas that actually matter: SEO, security, speed, and accessibility. Think of it as a checkup for your most important marketing asset.

The short version: Your website health score tells you whether your site is helping or hurting your business. Most small business websites score between 45-70 — which means they're leaving money on the table.

Why Should You Care About Your Score?

Your website isn't just a digital brochure. For most businesses, it's the first interaction potential customers have with your brand. A low health score means:

  • Lost search rankings — Google uses page speed, mobile-friendliness, and security as ranking factors. A slow, insecure site gets buried.
  • Lost customers — 53% of mobile users leave a page that takes longer than 3 seconds to load (Google).
  • Lost trust — A "Not Secure" warning in the browser kills credibility instantly.
  • Legal risk — ADA accessibility lawsuits hit over 8,600 businesses in 2024 alone.

How Is a Website Health Score Calculated?

A comprehensive health score isn't just one metric — it's a composite across multiple categories. Here's how Summit Web Audit's scoring works:

🔍 SEO (Search Engine Optimization)

Does your site have proper title tags, meta descriptions, heading structure, canonical URLs, structured data, and a sitemap? These determine whether Google can find, understand, and rank your content.

🔒 Security

Is HTTPS enabled and properly configured? Are security headers set (Content-Security-Policy, X-Frame-Options, etc.)? Is there mixed content? These protect your visitors and your reputation.

⚡ Performance

How fast does your page load? Are images optimized? Is compression enabled? Is the HTML lean or bloated? Speed directly impacts bounce rate and conversions.

♿ Accessibility

Do images have alt text? Are forms labeled? Is there a skip navigation link? Do you have ARIA landmarks? These make your site usable for everyone — and keep you compliant with ADA guidelines.

Each check in each category is weighted, and your final score is calculated out of 100. Our scanner currently runs 18 automated checks across these four areas.

What's a Good Score?

80–100: Excellent ✅

Your website is well-optimized. You're ahead of most competitors. Focus on content strategy and conversion optimization.

50–79: Needs Work ⚠️

There are meaningful issues to fix. You're likely losing traffic or customers due to specific, fixable problems. This is where most small business sites land.

0–49: Critical ❌

Your site has serious problems — missing HTTPS, broken SEO basics, major accessibility gaps. Every day without fixes is costing you business.

Common Issues That Tank Your Score

After scanning hundreds of small business websites, here are the most common problems we see:

  1. Missing meta descriptions — Google shows a random snippet instead of your carefully crafted message. Easy fix, huge SEO impact.
  2. No HTTPS or mixed content — Your SSL cert might be installed, but if even one image loads over HTTP, browsers show warnings.
  3. Missing image alt text — Bad for SEO, bad for accessibility, and it takes 30 seconds per image to fix.
  4. No security headers — Most small business sites have zero security headers. One server config change can fix this.
  5. Slow page speed — Uncompressed images and no server-side compression are the usual culprits.

How Often Should You Check?

Monthly at minimum. Your score can change when:

  • Plugins or themes update (can break things silently)
  • SSL certificates expire
  • New pages are added without proper meta tags
  • Your hosting provider changes server settings
  • Third-party scripts slow down your load time

After any major redesign, CMS migration, or hosting change, run a scan immediately.

FAQ

What is a good website health score?

A score of 80-100 is excellent and means your site is well-optimized across SEO, security, speed, and accessibility. Most small business websites score between 45-70, which means there are real improvements to make.

Is a website health score the same as Google PageSpeed?

No. Google PageSpeed only measures performance (loading speed). A website health score is broader — it includes SEO, security, and accessibility too. PageSpeed is one input into a health score, not the whole picture.
